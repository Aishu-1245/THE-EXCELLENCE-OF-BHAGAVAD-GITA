Introduction

Why to read Gita?


The Bhagavad Gita offers the fundamentals of life. The book describes the excellence of God, the greatness of Dharma and the superiority of Karma. The Gita explains the profound sense of selfless service.

The great Acharya Shankara Bhagavatpadacharya, the reviver of Sanathana Dharma, spoke about the Bhagavad Gita in his Govinda Stotram. He mentioned that one who sincerely listens to and grasps the wisdom of the Gita, who drunk even a drop of the water of the Ganges, and who worships Krishna wholeheartedly, will be protected from the reach of Yama (the lord of death).

In the Gita, which consists of 18 chapters and 700 verses (shlokas), Lord Krishna explains the nature of reality, covering topics such as:

Krishna explains in Gita about

  • Who is Human?
  • Who is God?
  • What is Karma?
  • What is Kalaswarupam(time)?
  • What is Nature?

VOICE OF THE GITA

The 700 shlokas are spoken by four individuals:

  • Lord Krishna: 574 Shlokas
  • Arjuna: 84 Shlokas
  • Sanjaya: 41 Shlokas
  • Dhritarashtra: 1 Shloka

Key Concepts

Dharma (Duty)

The principle of cosmic order and one's righteous duty. Krishna emphasizes performing one's own dharma selflessly.

Karma (Action)

The law of cause and effect. The Gita teaches the concept of Nishkama Karma, or action without attachment to the results.

Moksha (Liberation)

The ultimate goal of spiritual liberation from the cycle of birth and death (samsara), achieved through wisdom and devotion.

The Yogas

The paths to spiritual realization: Bhakti (devotion), Jnana (knowledge), Karma (action), and Dhyana (meditation).

Bhaja Govindam (Verse 1)

భగవద్గీతా కించిదధీతా గంగాజల-లవకణికా పీతా ।

సకృదపి యేన మురారిసమర్చా క్రియతే తస్య యమేన న చర్చా

Meaning: who has studied even a little of the Bhagavad Gita, drunk even a drop of the water of the Ganges, and worshipped Murari (Krishna) even once, will not have to argue with Yama (the god of death)
